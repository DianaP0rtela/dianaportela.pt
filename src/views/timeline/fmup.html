At the beginning of my residency in Public Health I started taking some online degrees to expand my knowledge on statistics, programming in R, python and data science, and this was when I felt the most satisfied in a long time. In fact, I realized the importance of quality information in clinical practice, the power it has, and thus, I can have by mastering these skills.

Latter on, in 2019, I started a PhD in Health Data Science (Specialty: Health Intelligent Data) at the Faculty of Medicine of the University of Porto (Porto, Portugal). My project is mostly a cluster analysis entitled “How can multimorbidity impact on hospitalizations clinical and economic outcomes? A case study on asthma”. My project was also part of the EIT Health PhD Label – Ageing at EIT PhD School 2020 (sponsored by European Innovation and Technology)
